##### Abstract

This study investigates the factors that contribute to difficulties that firms face when trying to recruit
new employees. We match firm surveys to firm’s tax records and administrative data to see how
factors such as industry, location, company size, and employment characteristics affect recruitment
difficulties. Our results show that about 10% of the variance in recruitment difficulties can be
explained by these observable factors, and up to 14% when considering difficulties from the previous
year. However, most of the difficulties encountered by companies are due to factors not captured by
our data, which may be related to internal characteristics of the company such as management quality
and the recruitment process.
